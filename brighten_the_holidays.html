The Salvation Army’s mission shines brightest at Christmastime.

The Salvation Army celebrates the birth of Christ by spreading His hope, love and joy to the less fortunate members of society. In fact, our gift-giving programs and fundraising initiatives are a direct answer to God’s calling to care for the poor, hungry, sick, and displaced. From physical needs such as grocery, utility and gift assistance, to emotional experiences including holiday events and traditions, to spiritual guidance that provides healing and hope, we work to assist the whole person through gifts that serve the body, mind and soul.

The Salvation Army brings happy holidays to many through:

Angel Tree Program

Each year, we help secure gifts of new clothing and toys for over one million needy children who wouldn’t otherwise get Christmas presents. These “angels” are adopted by anonymous donors as part of a growing Christmas tradition that often blesses the givers as much as the recipients.

Grocery and Food Assistance

The Salvation Army helps ensure that needy families, struggling seniors, and the homeless have something to eat over the holidays. Through sit-down Christmas dinners, meal delivery programs, and free food pantries, we bring the gift of sustenance to many.

Bill-pay Assistance

To help offset the financial burdens associated with winter heating costs and holiday expenses, we help struggling households pay basic utilities. This empowers families to stay current with their bills while maintaining valuable self-dignity and stability.

Holiday Events

Each December, we spread the joy of Christmas through traditions such as Bellringing, gifting programs, nursing home events, hospital visits, meal-delivery programs, and clothing and toy donations. These services provide relief and hope to shut-ins, the homeless, needy families and children of prisoners.
